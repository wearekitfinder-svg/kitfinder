The most collectible Brazil kits

The 1970 World Cup shirt — worn by Pelé, Jairzinho and Rivelino in what many consider the greatest team of all time — is the holy grail of football shirt collecting. Original examples from Mexico 1970 are extraordinarily rare and worth tens of thousands.

The 1994 USA World Cup shirt — Brazil's fourth title, won on penalties against Italy — is the most popular Brazil vintage shirt on the market. Romário and Bebeto's iconic baby-rocking celebration made this the most watched World Cup final ever. The Umbro shirt is widely collected.

The 2002 Korea/Japan World Cup shirt is Ronaldo's shirt — his comeback to become the tournament's top scorer with eight goals is one of sport's greatest stories. The Nike shirt is highly sought after.

The 1982 Spain World Cup shirt — featuring perhaps the greatest team never to win the World Cup: Zico, Sócrates, Falcão and Junior — is collected by connoisseurs worldwide.

Key facts

  • Brazil have won the FIFA World Cup five times — 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994 and 2002.
  • The 1970 Brazil team is widely regarded as the greatest international team in football history.
  • Brazil are the only nation to have appeared in every FIFA World Cup finals tournament.
  • Brazil's distinctive yellow shirt has been worn since 1954.
