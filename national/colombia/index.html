The most collectible Colombia kits

The 1990 Italy World Cup shirt — Colombia's return to the World Cup after 28 years, featuring the iconic Carlos Valderrama with his magnificent afro — is the most sought-after Colombia vintage shirt. The Umbro-manufactured yellow shirt with blue and red trim is instantly recognisable.

The 1994 USA World Cup shirt is Colombia's most tragic shirt. Tipped by many as potential winners and featuring Valderrama, Asprilla and the unfortunate Andrés Escobar — who scored an own goal that contributed to their elimination and was tragically murdered on his return home — this shirt carries enormous emotional weight.

The 2014 Brazil World Cup shirt — Colombia's best World Cup performance in decades, featuring a young James Rodríguez winning the Golden Boot — is the most popular modern Colombia collector piece. The Adidas shirt with its distinctive tricolour design is widely collected.

Carlos Valderrama is Colombia's most iconic player and his shirts from any era — 1990, 1994, 1998 World Cups — are the most individually collected Colombia player shirts.

Key facts

  • Colombia have participated in six FIFA World Cups — 1962, 1990, 1994, 1998, 2014 and 2018.
  • Carlos Valderrama is Colombia's most capped player and most recognisable football figure.
  • Colombia's 5-0 defeat of Argentina in Buenos Aires in 1993 (Valderrama era) is one of the most shocking results in South American football.
  • James Rodríguez's goal against Uruguay at the 2014 World Cup won the FIFA Puskás Award for goal of the year.
