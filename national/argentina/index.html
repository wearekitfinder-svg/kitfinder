The most collectible Argentina kits

The 1986 World Cup shirt — worn by Diego Maradona when he carried Argentina to the title in Mexico, scoring both the Hand of God and the Goal of the Century against England — is the single most iconic football shirt ever made. Original Le Coq Sportif examples from that tournament are extraordinarily rare and extremely valuable.

The 1978 World Cup winning shirt (Le Coq Sportif, worn on home soil in Buenos Aires) is even rarer. Argentina defeated Holland in the final in a patriotic frenzy — shirts from this tournament are museum pieces.

The 2022 Qatar World Cup shirt — Messi's long-awaited World Cup triumph and his crowning as the greatest player of all time — has already become hugely collectible. Original Adidas player-issue shirts from this tournament are widely sought after.

The 1990s and 2000s Adidas era produced a series of elegant Argentina shirts. The 1994 USA World Cup shirt (worn by Maradona before his drug test disqualification), the 1998 France World Cup and the 2006 Germany World Cup shirts are all popular with collectors.

Key facts

  • Argentina have won the FIFA World Cup three times — 1978, 1986 and 2022.
  • The 1986 World Cup shirt worn by Maradona is the most valuable football shirt ever auctioned.
  • Le Coq Sportif supplied Argentina in 1978 and 1986 — their shirts are now the rarest in South American football.
  • Adidas have been Argentina's primary kit supplier since the early 1990s.
  • Argentina's Copa América record includes 16 titles — the most of any nation.
