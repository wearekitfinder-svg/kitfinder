The most collectible Liverpool kits

The 1986-87 Candy home shirt — worn by Rush, Dalglish and Barnes — is one of the most beloved Liverpool shirts of all time. Simple, elegant and from Liverpool's dominant era of English football.

The 1984 European Cup winning shirt (plain red with Crown Paints sponsor) is one of Liverpool's rarest and most historically important shirts. Original examples are extremely hard to find in good condition.

The 2005 Istanbul Champions League shirt — worn in the miraculous comeback against AC Milan — is the most popular modern Liverpool jersey. Worn by Gerrard, Carragher, Alonso and Baros during football's greatest comeback.

John Barnes, Ian Rush and Robbie Fowler shirts are the most collected player shirts from Liverpool's vintage era. Any shirt with these names on the back attracts considerable interest from collectors.

Liverpool kits by decade

  • 1970s-80s: Umbro supplied Liverpool through their dominant era. Crown Paints-sponsored kits from this time are extremely collectible.
  • Late 1980s-90s: Candy and then Adidas. The Adidas kits of the early 1990s are popular with collectors.
  • 2000s: Reebok then Adidas supplied Liverpool. The Istanbul shirt (2004-05) is the standout jersey of this decade.
  • 2010s-present: Warrior then New Balance then Nike. Modern collectors focus on the 2019-20 title-winning shirt.
