The most collectible Juventus kits

The 1980s Kappa era — covering Michel Platini's three consecutive Ballon d'Or years and the 1985 European Cup — is the golden age for Juventus shirt collectors. Any original Kappa Juventus shirt from this decade is considered rare and highly valuable.

Roberto Baggio shirts from his time at Juventus (1990-95) are among the most collectible player shirts in Italian football. His magical dribbling and goals — combined with his tragic missed penalty at USA 94 — make any Baggio shirt instantly iconic.

The 1995-96 Champions League winning kit — worn in the penalty shootout victory over Ajax — is one of the most sought-after modern Juventus shirts. Del Piero's name on this shirt is among the most recognised in Serie A collecting.

Alessandro Del Piero shirts span three decades and are universally collected. His 2005-06 Serie A title shirt (from the season later stripped due to Calciopoli) carries particular historical significance.

Juventus kits by decade

  • 1970s-80s: Kappa supplied Juventus and produced their most iconic shirts. The Platini-era kits are the most sought-after.
  • 1990s: Kappa continued through much of this decade. The Champions League winning kits of 1996 are the standout collector pieces.
  • 2000s: Lotto took over and produced some elegant designs. Del Piero's later years fall in this era.
  • 2010s-present: Nike and then Adidas supply Juventus. The nine consecutive Serie A title shirts (2012-20) are popular with modern collectors.
