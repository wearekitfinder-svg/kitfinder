The most collectible PSG kits

The Daniel Hechter-designed hooped shirt — a blue and red vertical stripe design created by the famous fashion designer in the early 1970s — is PSG's most iconic historical kit. Any original example from this era is extremely rare and highly collectible.

The 1990s Nike era with the Champion sponsor is the most popular period for PSG collectors. George Weah's PSG shirts from 1988-92 are among the most sought-after, especially following his 1995 Ballon d'Or win.

The Ronaldinho era shirts (2001-03) are widely collected. His stunning debut season and the 2003 move to Barcelona make PSG shirts from this period historically significant.

The QSI takeover era (2011-present) brought global superstars and new kit designs. Zlatan Ibrahimovic's four PSG seasons produced some of the most collected modern French football shirts.

PSG kits by decade

  • 1970s: The Hechter hooped design was revolutionary and is now PSG's most iconic historical shirt.
  • 1980s-90s: Nike became kit supplier. The Champion-sponsored kits with their distinctive designs are very collectible.
  • 2000s: Nike continue. The early 2000s Ronaldinho shirts are the standout collector pieces.
  • 2010s-present: The QSI era. Jordan Brand collaborations and the Neymar/Mbappe shirts dominate modern collecting.
