The most collectible Arsenal kits

Arsenal have produced some of English football's most recognisable shirts. The 1991-93 bruised banana yellow away shirt — manufactured by Adidas with a bold yellow and black design — is one of the most iconic away kits in football history and now commands premium prices at auction.

The JVC-sponsored home shirts of the late 1980s and 1990s are the heart of most Arsenal collections. The 1988-89 Adidas home shirt, worn when Arsenal's Michael Thomas scored in the final minute to steal the title at Anfield, is one of the most historically significant shirts in English football.

The 2003-04 Invincibles season shirt is the most sought-after modern Arsenal jersey. Worn by the only team to go an entire Premier League season unbeaten, original match-worn and player-issue shirts regularly sell for several hundred pounds.

Player shirts are also hugely collectible. Dennis Bergkamp, Thierry Henry and Patrick Vieira shirts — especially from the late 1990s and early 2000s — attract serious collectors worldwide. Henry's record-breaking goal-scoring seasons make his name on the back of any shirt highly desirable.

Arsenal kits by decade

  • 1970s: Arsenal wore Umbro and later Admiral kits. The 1971 Double-winning kit with its simple red and white design is a rare find.
  • 1980s: JVC arrived as shirt sponsor in 1982. Adidas supplied the kits through most of this decade, producing some of the most collected shirts in English football.
  • 1990s: Nike took over in 1994. The iconic 1994-96 black away shirt is one of the most popular Arsenal shirts among collectors.
  • 2000s: The move to the Emirates Stadium brought new kits. The Invincibles shirt (2003-04) and the Centenary kit (1986-87) are the highlights of this era.
  • 2010s onwards: Puma (2014-19) and then Adidas (2019-present) supply the kits. Vintage fans focus on the earlier decades.
